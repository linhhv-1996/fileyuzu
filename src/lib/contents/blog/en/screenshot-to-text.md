Screenshots are commonly saved as PNG, JPG, JPEG, or WebP depending on your device and app.

## Screenshot OCR Works Best With Clear Text

OCR accuracy depends on how readable the screenshot is. If the text is crisp and large, the result is usually good. If the screenshot is tiny, blurry, or compressed, mistakes are more likely.

OCR may struggle with:

* tiny text

## Tips to Get Better Text from Screenshots

### Capture Only the Text Area

Do not screenshot the entire screen if you only need one paragraph or one error message. Cropping around the text gives OCR less noise to process.

### Use the Original Screenshot

Avoid sending the screenshot through messaging apps before OCR. Many chat apps compress images and make small text harder to read.

### Zoom In Before Taking the Screenshot

If the text is small, zoom in first. Larger text produces better OCR results.

### Avoid Motion Blur

If you are taking a screenshot from a video, pause on a clear frame. Motion blur can make letters unreadable.

### Use High Contrast Mode When Possible

Dark text on a light background, or light text on a dark background, is easier to detect than low-contrast text.

### Review Important Text Manually

OCR can make small mistakes, especially with numbers, codes, names, and symbols. Always proofread important output.

## Limitations of Screenshot OCR

OCR is helpful, but it is not perfect.

### It May Misread Similar Characters

Characters like `O` and `0`, `I` and `1`, or `B` and `8` can be confused. This matters for codes, passwords, serial numbers, and IDs.

### It May Break Line Formatting

OCR may insert line breaks where the screenshot wraps text visually. You may need to clean up the result before using it.

### It May Struggle with Tables

Screenshots of tables can lose structure. OCR may extract the text but not preserve columns perfectly.

### It Cannot Read Hidden Text

OCR only reads visible text. If part of the text is cut off, covered, blurred, or too small, the tool cannot reconstruct it accurately.

### It May Struggle with Decorative Fonts

Stylized text, low-contrast graphics, curved text, or text over images may reduce accuracy.
